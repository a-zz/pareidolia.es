Halftone Clouds

I like clouds, op-art, and the possibilities and tradition of halftone in pop-art*, why not putting it all together?

I've also come to know (and enjoy) Perlin noise. That's a clever algorithm for generating random noise that changes smoothly over any number of dimensions. For the sketch shown above, Perlin noise is computed for x, y and t dimensions, as clouds have random spatial density changing randomly over time. p5.js has a builtin function for Perlin noise, so it's as easy as ABC (although the scales for the dimensions involved must be carefully tweaked to get a naturally-looking effect). You may have notice that the clouds above travel slowly sideways, although the underlying dots have static positions; that's simply achieved by increasing slightly over time the value of the x dimension used to compute noise.
